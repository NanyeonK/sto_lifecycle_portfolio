# Main Research Question

## The Question

Does residential housing tokenization with continuous fractional
ownership and explicit service-rights coupling raise lifetime utility
relative to a binary own/rent regime, and through which channel does the
welfare gain operate over the lifecycle?

## Motivation

Tokenized residential real-estate platforms (RealT and others) are
growing at a 21-27 percent CAGR. The household-finance literature has
not yet asked what fraction of welfare gain from such institutional
structures comes from removing the indivisibility friction versus from
removing the bundling of housing service and housing asset. The two
channels imply different normative recommendations for tokenization
design and regulation.

## Target Claim

Tokenized residential housing with continuous `theta in [0, 1]` and a
rent-saving wedge `delta = r_S - d_T > 0` raises lifetime utility above
a binary own/rent baseline, with the welfare gain decomposable into:

- a rent-saving channel (the new contribution), and
- an asset-allocation channel (recovered by the REIT-access falsification
  limit).

The rent-saving channel accounts for the structural distinction from
REIT-access lifecycle benchmarks. Its magnitude rises in the
indivisibility wedge and the labor-income / housing-return correlation.

## Intended Contribution

A closed lifecycle welfare decomposition that separates the
indivisibility-relaxation channel (already studied) from the
service-rights coupling channel (new). The decomposition is the central
exhibit. The same decomposition serves as the in-model defense against
the "tokens are just REITs" referee dismissal.

## What This Paper Should Not Claim

- Causal welfare effects of real-world tokenization adoption.
- General-equilibrium housing-price impact of widespread tokenization.
- Predictions about specific platforms' returns or fees.
- Investment advice regarding any STO token.
- Empirical identification of `delta`; the calibration is
  design-conditional.

## Target Journal

- Primary: Review of Financial Studies (RFS).
- Backup: Journal of Finance (JF), Management Science (MS).

Audience: finance theory, household finance, real-estate finance,
tokenization design.
